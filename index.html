Story of My Hero Academia

In a world where nearly 80% of the population has developed a unique power called a "Quirk", society has adapted to the existence of heroes and villains. Izuku Midoriya, a Quirkless boy, idolizes pro heroes and dreams of becoming one himself. His life changes when he meets All Might, the Symbol of Peace, who passes on his Quirk, "One For All", to Izuku. With this newfound power, Izuku enrolls at U.A. High School, where he learns to fight, protect, and grow into a true hero alongside his friends.

U.A. High School

U.A. High School is Japan's top academy for aspiring heroes. Located in Musutafu, it is known for its rigorous hero course, advanced facilities, and renowned faculty. Students undergo intense combat training, academic education, and real-world hero simulations. Classes like 1-A are home to standout students such as Shoto Todoroki, Katsuki Bakugo, and Ochaco Uraraka. The school also frequently faces villain threats, adding real danger to its prestigious name. Yet, through all this, it remains the cornerstone of the future generation of heroes.

What Are Quirks?

Quirks are special superhuman abilities that individuals are born with. These powers can range from elemental control, telekinesis, shape-shifting, to more bizarre traits. Quirks are inherited genetically, often a combination of both parents'. However, not all Quirks are suitable for hero work. Some are passive, while others are highly destructive. The government classifies and regulates Quirks through licensing systems. In "My Hero Academia", mastering your Quirk is the key to becoming a pro hero or a fearsome villain.
